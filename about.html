My Journey

My fascination with technology began in my childhood, sparked by the magic of a simple Game Boy. I was mesmerized by how much capability and how many complex worlds could be packed into such a small device. Driven by the realization that it was all governed by computation and electronics, I chose to specialize in Computer Science early on during my school years.

However, I quickly realized that school only scratched the surface. To truly understand how things work under the hood, I pursued a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering. This allowed me to bridge the gap between software and hardware, mastering the physical components that bring code to life.

While my Bachelor's degree taught me how to solve isolated engineering problems, I wanted to understand how to architect and manage large-scale, complex projects. This ambition led me to a Master's program in Systems Engineering and Engineering Management. During this time, the rapid rise of Artificial Intelligence completely captivated me. I tailored my Master's thesis precisely to this intersection, focusing on the modeling of discrete systems utilizing novel logic layers.

That research was a profound turning point, paving the way for my Ph.D. dissertation. Moving into the complexities of real-world applications, my doctoral research focuses on multimodal learning and multimodal fusion within industrial environments. Modern industry generates vast amounts of heterogeneous data, from visual and acoustic signals to complex sensor readings. My work aims to intelligently fuse these diverse data streams to create robust models. Crucially, I am investigating multimodal fault tolerance to ensure these AI systems remain reliable and resilient, even when individual data sources or sensors fail in harsh industrial contexts.
